Before You Reach Out — A Few Things That Help

  • Have your device charged and turned on, and know its passcode if it has one.
  • Try to describe what happened in your own words — for example, "the screen went black" or "I tapped something and now I can't find my photos."
  • If it's easy for you, a photo of the screen helps me see exactly what you see.
  • Find a quiet spot to talk or video chat, so it's easier to hear each other.
Try It Yourself

Quick Tips & Common Fixes

A few things you can often solve in under a minute, no call needed. If it doesn't work, that's exactly what I'm here for.

My screen is too dim, or the text is too small

On most phones and tablets, swipe down from the top of the screen to open quick settings, where you'll usually find a brightness slider. For text size, look in Settings under "Display" or "Accessibility" for an option like "Text Size" or "Larger Text."

Still stuck? Call me — this takes two minutes together.

My phone keeps buzzing with notifications

Notifications can usually be turned down app by app in Settings under "Notifications." If one particular app is the culprit (like a game or news app), you can turn its notifications off without affecting the rest of your phone.

Still stuck? I can walk you through it by phone.

I can't hear my phone ring or my videos

Check the physical volume buttons on the side of your device first, and make sure it isn't switched to silent mode (on iPhones, this is a small switch above the volume buttons). Then check the volume slider inside Settings > Sounds.

Still stuck? Bring it to the next Tech Help Bar or give me a call.

I can't find a photo I know I took

Open your Photos app and check if you're looking in the right album — "Recents" or "All Photos" usually shows everything. Photos are sometimes accidentally moved to a "Deleted" or "Hidden" album, which can also be checked.

Still stuck? Text me a photo of your screen and I'll help you find it.

I forgot my password or can't log in

Look for a "Forgot Password" link on the login screen — most services will let you reset it using your email or phone number. Avoid guessing too many times, as some accounts lock temporarily after several wrong attempts.

Still stuck? This one's often easier over a video call — let's do it together.

An app or update is asking me to do something confusing

It's always okay to pause and ask before tapping anything you're unsure about, especially if it's asking for a password, payment, or personal information. When in doubt, don't tap — just reach out to me first.

Still stuck, or something feels off? Call me before doing anything else.

Good to Know

Common Questions

Is this really free?

Yes. This is a free service for Atria Senior Living residents. There is never a charge.

What if I don't know how to take a screenshot or send a text?

That's completely alright — just call me instead, and I'll walk you through it, or wait and bring your device to the next Tech Help Bar.

Do I need to bring my device to the Tech Help Bar?

You can bring your phone, tablet, or laptop if you have a question about it, or just bring your questions — either way works.

What kinds of problems can be solved remotely?

Many simple things: adjusting text size or volume, showing you how to make a call or send a photo, or explaining a confusing screen. More involved issues are easiest to solve together in person at the Tech Help Bar.
